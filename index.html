Public Data for Medicare Advantage Bid & Margin Forecasting

A curated catalog of public & low-cost datasets useful for modeling MA membership, bids, revenue, cost, and plan-level margin. Grouped by what they help you model.

Links & downloadable spans byte-verified June 2026 — click any dataset row to see its per-year file URLs. Unless tagged otherwise, every source is free to download.
Most of MA's public data is supply-side (what CMS pays, what care costs); the harder gaps are realized plan economics, the demand/choice side, and the “true” underlying risk pool. This catalog spans all of it. Each entry notes the grain (the join key you'll actually use) and the access tier. A green ✓ span badge marks the years we actually downloaded & byte-validated; △ = no clean bulk file, ⊘ = paywall/login/key.

Verified dead ends

What does NOT exist publicly (so you can stop looking)

Each of these was checked on the actual pages (June 2026). The obvious-sounding source either doesn't exist at usable detail, or is gated behind HPMS / a data-use agreement.

CTM complaint rates (monthly/quarterly) — not public at any cadence; plans see their own in HPMS. The annual star-ratings complaints measure is the public ceiling.
Health Outcomes Survey (HOS) by contract — contract IDs are blinded in the public files AND the DUA tier; pre-October results go only to the plans via HPMS. The star tables are the first public sighting.
IRE (independent review) appeals by contract — quarterly fact sheets are national aggregates; no H-numbers anywhere. The stars appeals measures already carry the IRE-derived signal.
Part C/D Reporting Requirements (grievances, appeals volumes) — a Limited Data Set: DUA + fee, ~2-year lag. The free grievance/appeals rates inside the star and display files carry most of the same signal.
County flu vaccination (CDC FluVaxView) — no county download product exists; the county Medicare flu rate lives in MMD / County Health Rankings instead.
BRFSS SMART county estimates — ~4% of counties per year and no defensible 65+ cut; use PLACES or MMD.
Dartmouth Atlas — discontinued; frozen at 2019 data permanently.
NCQA report cards as a stars preview — same-HEDIS-year ratings post ~3 weeks before CMS stars, but with no contract IDs, rating-scale only, and restrictive licensing.
Contract-level disability / dual percentages — CMS computes them internally; the public proxies are the LIS files, the SNP report, and the banded CAI adjustment categories inside the star zips.
State insurance-department complaint indexes for MA — the whole family fails on federal preemption: MA member complaints route to CMS (CTM/1-800-MEDICARE), not state DOIs. Verified: NAIC CIS has no free bulk export (per-company search UI; bulk products are paid) and is jurisdictionally thin on MA; Texas TDI's excellent open dataset has zero Medicare rows (no MA coverage line); California DMHC's IMR/complaint data excludes MA (appeals go to the federal IRE); NY DFS's health-insurer complaint rankings explicitly exclude Medicare.
Drug-shortage history (full event archive) — ASHP's database (richer than FDA's) is paid-license only, RSS is current-snapshot only; FDA's legacy shortage site is decommissioned (404) and the HealthData.gov export is a stale 2021 stub with zero columns. openFDA (Bucket 3) is the only live free feed, and it purges resolved records after ~1 year — a complete historical start/end panel does not exist publicly; Wayback reconstruction is the only route.
Urban hospital-closure registry — does not exist publicly: GAO anonymizes the hospitals in its closure reports, the AHA Annual Survey is paid, Becker’s lists are paywalled and non-systematic. The UNC Sheps registry (Bucket 5) is rural-only; an unbiased national closure series would need the CMS Provider of Services termination file (feasible follow-up).
Deep app-review history — the iTunes RSS review feed is hard-capped at the 500 most-recent reviews and there is no historical endpoint for aggregate ratings, so multi-year review panels for popular carrier apps cannot be reconstructed (Wayback snapshots of store pages are the only partial salvage). Google Play has no documented public API; deep Play history requires ToS-gray internal endpoints.
Carrier sentiment as a CAHPS-star lead (Reddit / GDELT news tone / Wikipedia attention / thelayoff) — screened June 2026 (carrier-year sentiment in year Y vs CAHPS-family star change at star year Y+2). All four NO-GO: Reddit complaint-share rho +0.09 (right sign, tiny, flips negative at Y+3); GDELT negative news tone rho −0.04 with zero down-vs-up separation; Wikipedia pageviews rho −0.10; thelayoff post volume sign-flips by lag and is confounded with Internet-Archive snapshot density. Lawful routes confirmed and reusable (PullPush archive API for Reddit; GDELT 2.0 DOC API; Wikimedia pageviews REST; Internet-Archive CDX for thelayoff) — the data exists and is collectible, the SIGNAL does not lead. Indeed reviews (the best-fit front-line CSR/grievance population) were the strongest mechanistic candidate and got a FULL fair test (June 2026): the resumable Wayback fetch completed all 9 carriers (391 snapshots, 6,777 dated reviews; 3-era parser, IA rate-limiting handled by give-up-and-resume). NO-GO — the cross-carrier screen is null on both the aggregate carrier rating (rho −0.04) AND the sharpest cut, the mean rating of front-line CSR/claims/grievance-appeals reviews per carrier-year (rho +0.02/+0.10, no down-vs-up separation). Even the workers literally doing the appeals/CSR jobs don't lead the CAHPS-family star measures.
insurance-forums.com (senior-insurance agent board) — the single richest agent-side signal (dated, carrier-named call-center/claims/commission complaints) but RIGHTS-RESERVED: robots.txt names ClaudeBot in an explicit Disallow + Content-Signals ai-train=no as an EU-2019/790 rights reservation, Cloudflare-enforced (403). NOT collected. Only lawful routes: site-operator permission/license, or a human reading threads. Recorded so future sessions don’t re-attempt automated collection.
Glassdoor / Blind employee reviews — both explicitly disallow Anthropic crawlers (robots) and Cloudflare-gate live; Glassdoor-via-Wayback gives an annual sub-rating series but stops dead mid-2023, and Blind’s payer content is software-engineers (wrong population for call-center/ops signal). Indeed is the only employee-review channel with the right front-line population, parses from Wayback, and was fully tested (above) — null even on the front-line-role review ratings.
Stock options (put/call IV & skew) as a star lead — assessed (June 2026), NO-GO for backtest + low value forward. Backtest is data-walled: no free historical option-chain/IV source (Yahoo options endpoint dead; CBOE put/call history 403s; Alpha Vantage historical-options needs a key at 25 req/day; quality IV is paid — CBOE DataShop / ORATS / OptionMetrics). CURRENT chains ARE free (CBOE delayed-quotes JSON, e.g. cdn.cboe.com/api/global/delayed_quotes/options/HUM.json — 2,052 contracts w/ IV), so a FORWARD Oct-catalyst IV gauge is buildable — but it carries only magnitude (uncertainty), never direction, and the share-price screen already showed the underlying does not anticipate ratings (preview-drift rho −0.03), so option skew almost certainly doesn't either. The 8-K disclosure is the primary leak; everything priced off it is a lagged echo.
Share price as a star-rating lead — screened (June 2026, 48 public-parent-years, abnormal vs XLV): the trailing-12-month return is WRONG-signed as a feature (rho −0.15); August–September preview-window drift carries nothing (rho −0.03; big-down vs flat vs big-up years all drift +2–3%); only the October publication REACTION is real (rho +0.22). The market prices star news but does not anticipate it — the pre-publication information arrives via discrete 8-K disclosures (see the EDGAR row), not continuous drift. Humana’s Sept-2024 −13.7% drift before the Oct-2 H5216 8-K is one suggestive case in 46, an anecdote.
Prediction markets (Polymarket / Kalshi) on star ratings — verified empty (June 2026): no Medicare Advantage star-rating, CMS-rulemaking, or insurer-quality markets exist on either platform; the closest are generic “will HUM/UNH beat quarterly earnings” markets. Nothing to read. (If such markets ever list, the Aug/Sep plan-preview asymmetry would make them genuinely informative — worth a re-check each September.)
MA MLR QIA spend as a quality lead — exists (detailed-year §4 lines) but tested NOT predictive of subsequent star movement (rho ≈ 0 within rating band; negative level-correlation = remediation spend). Recorded so nobody re-mines it.
