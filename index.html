Public Data for Medicare Advantage Bid & Margin Forecasting

A curated catalog of public & low-cost datasets useful for modeling MA membership, bids, revenue, cost, and plan-level margin. Grouped by what they help you model.

Links verified June 2026 · Unless tagged otherwise, every source is free to download.
Most of MA's public data is supply-side (what CMS pays, what care costs); the harder gaps are realized plan economics, the demand/choice side, and the “true” underlying risk pool. This catalog spans all of it. Each entry notes the grain (the join key you'll actually use) and the access tier.

Bucket 1

Plan bids, payment & margin

The core economics at contract / plan grain — the closest public windows onto what plans bid, what CMS actually pays, and realized medical-loss ratio.

DatasetWhat it is & why it helpsGrain · size · yearsAccess
MA Plan Payment Data (Part C/D) CMS's enrollment-weighted realized average A/B payment PMPM, rebate PMPM, and Part C risk score per plan — the realized counterpart to the bid. The single best free anchor for the revenue waterfall. contract-PBP + county ~52k plan-years · 2006–2024 FREE
MA Bid Pricing Data (BPT PUF) Extracts of the filled Bid Pricing Tool: each plan's projected bid, allowed/paid cost, admin load, rebate, and its own gain/(loss) margin line. The only public view of plan-projected economics. contract-PBP-segment ~CY2014–2021 (5-yr lag) FREE
Contract MLR Public Use File Reported medical-loss ratio plus revenue, claims, member-months and (detailed years) 19 supplemental-benefit line items + the Part D split. The real cost/revenue anchor — and the distribution of contract MLRs. Detailed years (2014–17, 2023; CMS published only a 5-column summary 2018–22) also carry the §4 quality-improvement (QIA) expense lines — tested as a star-rating leading indicator: NOT predictive (level-correlation negative: low-rated contracts spend more, i.e. remediation). contract ~166 cols · 2014–2023 FREE
MedPAC Data Book & Reports The authoritative national quantification of MA payment vs FFS, average rebate PMPM, and the payment-gap split into coding intensity vs favorable selection — with downloadable figure Excel. Best macro calibration anchor. national annual · 2007–2026 FREE
MA Ratebooks & Rate Announcement County benchmarks (the bid ceiling), the FFS standardized rates behind them, and the annual payment parameters (growth, normalization, coding-intensity, rebate % by star). The legal Apr-T inputs for next-year bids. county / national annual · 2000–2027 FREE
Rule calendar — Federal Register API + HPMS memos Every industry-wide star-rating shock 2022–2026 (better-of expiry, guardrails, Tukey, weight shifts, measure removals) was announced in a final rule 2–4 years ahead. The Federal Register API serves CMS rules as free JSON back to 1994 (same-day); Advance Notice / Rate Announcement live at stable per-year cms.gov PDFs (~Feb / Apr 1); the public HPMS weekly memo archive (~2015–, ZIPs of PDFs) carries the sub-regulatory star-ratings operational detail. Together: a machine-readable announced-methodology-changes calendar. event calendar 1994– / 2015– FREE
Bucket 2

Plan benefits, enrollment & quality

The plan attributes a forecaster needs as features — benefit design, who enrolled where, star quality, and member experience.

DatasetWhat it is & why it helpsGrain · size · yearsAccess
Plan Benefit Package (PBP) Data Full per-plan benefit design: deductibles, MOOP, per-service cost-sharing, and supplemental benefits (dental/vision/hearing/OTC/meals/transport/fitness). Published incl. forward years — a legal Aug-T feature. contract-PBP-segment annual incl. 2026 FREE
MA/Part D Enrollment & Landscape Monthly enrollment by contract-plan-county (CPSC), the consumer-facing Landscape (premiums/benefits), county penetration, and the year-over-year plan crosswalk (consolidations/terminations). The membership substrate. contract-PBP × county monthly · 2006– FREE
Star Ratings (incl. CAHPS & disenrollment) Every quality measure by contract — including the CAHPS member-experience composites (top-box 0–100) and the “Members Choosing to Leave the Plan” voluntary-disenrollment rate. Strong retention predictors (experience ↔ disenroll ≈ −0.4). contract annual · 2010–2026 FREE
Display Measures (pre-rating quality) Quality measures CMS tracks and publishes but does not rate (yet) — operational quality outside the stars, and a pipeline of future rated measures. Ships inside the Star Ratings zips through 2023 and as standalone display-measures zips from 2024. Heads-up: the 2019 file is tab-separated despite its .csv name. contract annual · 2012–2026 · ~40–58 measures/yr FREE
LIS Enrollment by Plan One workbook per year with BOTH total and low-income-subsidy enrollment per contract-PBP — the LIS share needs no external denominator. Published early in-year: the freshest public member-mix signal (the Plan Payment LICS series lags ~2 years). 2019 is missing from both CMS URL eras. A contract×county variant is also published. contract-PBP annual · 2012–2026 FREE
SNP Comprehensive Report Plan-level special-needs enrollment by SNP type (dual / chronic / institutional), monthly since 2008 — the public window on dual-eligible mix and its drift. Workbooks carry several alternative views of the same plans; pick one, never concatenate them. contract-plan · monthly 2008– FREE
Part D Formulary, Pharmacy Network & Pricing The complete public drug benefit: every drug on every plan's formulary with tier and prior-auth / step-therapy / quantity-limit flags, plus drug pricing and pharmacy networks. Joins to contract-plan via the plan-information file — build formulary breadth, tier richness, and UM intensity as Part D cost and plan-choice features. formulary → contract-PBP quarterly · ~2.6 GB/release FREE
Bucket 3

Cost, risk & utilization benchmarks

Local fee-for-service and provider data — the proxy MA medical cost tracks — plus the one public MA-specific utilization series.

DatasetWhat it is & why it helpsGrain · size · yearsAccess
Medicare Geographic Variation PUF County FFS standardized per-capita cost and utilization rates (admits, ED, readmits per 1,000) plus average HCC. The canonical county cost/utilization benchmark for the cost chain. county / state / national annual · 2007– FREE
MSSP County Expenditure & Risk PUF County per-capita FFS cost and CMS-HCC risk score split by ESRD / disabled / aged-dual / aged-nondual — a real coded-RAF anchor and a selection-by-segment signal (ESRD ~$93k vs aged-nondual ~$12k/yr). county × enrollment-type 2014–2019/24 FREE
HCRIS Hospital Cost Reports Provider-level realized total/operating margin, actual cost, cost-to-charge ratios, and Medicare/managed-care day mix. The only source with realized provider margin; county-mappable via CCN. provider (CCN) → county ~6.8k hosp · 2011– FREE
CMS Program Statistics — MA Utilization Encounter-data-derived MA utilization (IP/SNF/OP/physician, per-1,000) — the only public MA-specific utilization series, so you can check that MA use ≠ the FFS proxy. national / service-type 2016–2022 FREE
Provider Utilization & Payment PUFs FFS service-line detail: Physician/Other Practitioners, Part D Prescribers, Inpatient/Outpatient charges, post-acute. Builds local service-mix and unit-cost benchmarks. provider / state × code 2013– FREE
Mapping Medicare Disparities (MMD) County-level Medicare FFS rates for exactly the preventive-care territory the Star Ratings score: mammography, colorectal screening, flu vaccination, diabetes-care indicators, readmissions, ED visits — by age/sex/race/dual. Export is per-selection CSV (no bulk file); County Health Rankings redistributes the key measures in one national CSV per year. county annual · 2012– (~2-yr lag) · FFS only FREE
Care Compare — Unplanned Hospital Visits Hospital-level 30-day readmission and excess-days measures with county sitting right in the file — a clean county roll-up for local delivery-system quality (the readmission environment MA members actually use). Bulk CSV + a working datastore API; full-snapshot archive zips DO exist at a stable pattern from 2021 (next row) — an earlier note here saying otherwise is superseded. hospital → county 3-yr rolling window · refreshed ~annually FREE
Care Compare archive snapshots — HCAHPS & suite Full flat-file zips of ALL hospital CSVs (HCAHPS patient experience, overall stars, timely & effective care, readmissions) at …/archive/Hospitals/{YYYY}/hospitals_{MM}_{YYYY}.zip, verified 2021–2025 incl. July snapshots — so exact as-of-August joins. Home-health (HHCAHPS) and nursing-home siblings under the same pattern. County-aggregated HCAHPS is the most direct outside proxy for the CAHPS star measures. Adopted into the star-rating forecaster (exo round 2, June 2026) — part of a gate-passing +0.005 exact-accuracy gain. Pre-2021 vintages only via an unofficial mirror (github.com/asacarny/hospital-compare, 2004–2016). provider → county snapshots 2021– FREE
Part D Prescriber PUFs (Geography / Provider × Drug) By-Geography: state × drug claims/fills/beneficiaries with an LIS cost-share split, 2013–2024 — a clean adherence-exposure panel (state only, no county). By-Provider-and-Drug: NPI × drug; county derivable via the companion by-Provider file's zip5 (+ dual share, avg risk score, MAPD/LIS splits) → county × drug-class panel. ~17-month lag (year-Y data ≈ April Y+2): T−2 at an August-T clock. Files are multi-GB. state / NPI → county 2013–2024 FREE
openFDA Drug Shortages The only live machine-readable FDA shortage feed (API + one bulk JSON, daily refresh, free): start dates, status, therapeutic category — PDC-relevant classes (cardiovascular, endocrine) identifiable. HARD CAVEAT: it is a live database, not an archive — resolved shortages purge after ~1 year (only 30 Resolved records), so forward features are excellent but backtest history needs Wayback reconstruction. ASHP's richer database is paid-license only; the legacy FDA site and the HealthData.gov export are dead (see dead ends). national × drug class live · partial history to 2012 FREE
County pharmacy access — NPPES + PDP network files Two free routes to pharmacy-desert dynamics: (1) NPPES monthly files filtered to pharmacy taxonomy (3336*) → county pharmacy counts/closures; NBER mirrors monthly vintages 2017→current (closure dating needs the deactivation file — dead NPIs linger). (2) CMS monthly PDP formulary zips (2019–) include the pharmacy network file keyed by contract — in-network/preferred pharmacy counts per contract per county, a direct "getting needed drugs" feature. Census CBP (NAICS 446110/456110, 1986–2023) is the long-history county backfill (code break 2022, 2-yr lag). county / contract monthly 2017– / 2019– FREE

Bucket 5

County demographics, denominators & SDOH (the “true risk pool”)

County context that drives who enrolls and how sick a market is. The social-determinants layer is especially useful as the gap coded risk scores miss: SDOH-implied need minus coded RAF ≈ the under-diagnosis / favorable-selection wedge.

DatasetWhat it is & why it helpsGrain · size · yearsAccess
Census ACS — Health Insurance County 65+ population, Medicare-covered share, and age structure (tables B27001/C27006) — the membership denominator over which MA penetration is computed. county annual · 2008– FREE · KEY
Area Health Resources File (AHRF) One county file with 6,000+ variables — physician/specialist supply, hospital beds, facilities, demographics, rurality. A ready-made county market-structure feature pack. county ~3,235 cty · annual FREE
HRSA HPSA shortage designations County primary-care / mental-health shortage designations as one cumulative CSV (updated daily) with designation AND withdrawal dates — so historical as-of snapshots are reconstructable (designated ≤ date < withdrawn). Joins on county FIPS; a clean access-to-care signal. Caveat: HPSA score/FTE reflect the latest update only — use status + designation age as the time-resolved fields. Adopted (star forecaster exo round 2, June 2026). county FIPS live · dates → any as-of FREE
BLS QCEW — county healthcare staffing County × NAICS healthcare-sector (62, 621/622/623) establishment employment and wages, annual back to 1990 (+quarterly), free bulk zips at data.bls.gov/cew — workforce-tightness exposure for access/experience modeling. Annual Y publishes ~June Y+1. OEWS adds occupational detail (RNs, physicians) but is MSA-level only and its servers block automated fetches — marginal next to QCEW. county × NAICS annual 1990– · qtrly FREE
CDC PLACES Model-based county prevalence of 26 chronic conditions (diabetes, CHD, COPD, CKD…). Real measured morbidity at county grain — a strong “true need” signal independent of MA coding. county ~3,150 cty · 2020–25 FREE
CMS Medicare Chronic Conditions (CCW) Claims-measured county prevalence of 21 chronic conditions among Medicare FFS beneficiaries — incl. chronic kidney disease and heart failure that PLACES omits — plus a multiple-condition burden tail and FFS per-capita spend. The Medicare-specific complement to PLACES (model-based / all-adult); disease-resolved, so conditions map to drug classes for Part D / out-of-pocket modeling. FFS-only, so it reads the risk pool's shape, not the MA pool. county (FFS) ~3,144 cty · 2017 FREE
CDC WONDER / Census PEP County all-cause mortality and 65+ population denominators — a county morbidity/mortality proxy (and an independent population base). (Available via Census PEP when the WONDER API is unavailable.) county × age 1999– / 2011–24 FREE
County Health Rankings & Roadmaps A county-native SDOH bundle (poverty, income, uninsured, education, housing, premature death) split into outcomes vs upstream factors — purpose-built for a need-vs-risk analysis. One harmonized file. county ~3,150 cty · 2021–25 FREE
Social Deprivation Index (SDI) A clean 7-factor county deprivation composite (poverty, education, housing, no-car, non-employment) — free of ADI's distorting home-value term and built to predict unmet care need. county / ZCTA ~3,140 cty · ACS vintages FREE
USDA Food Environment Atlas County food insecurity, food access, SNAP, and obesity/diabetes. Food insecurity has the best-quantified SDOH→senior-Medicare-cost link (~$5.5k/yr) and ties to grocery-benefit modeling. county ~3,150 cty · to 2025 FREE
Census SAIPE + BLS LAUS Current-vintage county poverty/median income (SAIPE) + monthly/annual unemployment (LAUS) — the timely economic-stress layer that the 5-year ACS / deprivation vintages miss. county annual · 1989/1990– FREE · KEY
AHRQ SDOH Database One pre-compiled, documented county/ZIP/tract panel of social-determinants variables from ~44 sources (ACS, AHRF, CDC...) — five domains incl. transportation and healthcare access. Largely supersedes hand-assembling ACS pulls; dedupe against AHRF for supply fields. Site blocks automated fetchers (fine in a browser); a DataLumos mirror exists. county / ZIP / tract 2009–2023 FREE
CDC U.S. Diabetes Surveillance System County diagnosed-diabetes prevalence/incidence back to 2004 — the longest county diabetes trend available. Caveats: modeled small-area estimates (same BRFSS lineage as PLACES), all-adult not 65+, and export is click-per-indicator from the atlas (no bulk file). For the Medicare population the CMS Chronic Conditions file usually dominates; this earns its place only for the 2004+ trend depth. county annual · 2004– FREE
CDC SVI & UW ADI County/tract social-vulnerability and area-deprivation composites. Convenient SDOH indices — note ADI's home-value term distorts high-cost counties (prefer SDI for cost work); ADI needs a free login. ADI’s click-through license bars redistribution; a license-free standardized ADI lives in NaNDA on openICPSR. county / tract biennial / periodic FREE · REGISTER

Verified dead ends

What does NOT exist publicly (so you can stop looking)

Each of these was checked on the actual pages (June 2026). The obvious-sounding source either doesn't exist at usable detail, or is gated behind HPMS / a data-use agreement.

CTM complaint rates (monthly/quarterly) — not public at any cadence; plans see their own in HPMS. The annual star-ratings complaints measure is the public ceiling.
Health Outcomes Survey (HOS) by contract — contract IDs are blinded in the public files AND the DUA tier; pre-October results go only to the plans via HPMS. The star tables are the first public sighting.
IRE (independent review) appeals by contract — quarterly fact sheets are national aggregates; no H-numbers anywhere. The stars appeals measures already carry the IRE-derived signal.
Part C/D Reporting Requirements (grievances, appeals volumes) — a Limited Data Set: DUA + fee, ~2-year lag. The free grievance/appeals rates inside the star and display files carry most of the same signal.
County flu vaccination (CDC FluVaxView) — no county download product exists; the county Medicare flu rate lives in MMD / County Health Rankings instead.
BRFSS SMART county estimates — ~4% of counties per year and no defensible 65+ cut; use PLACES or MMD.
Dartmouth Atlas — discontinued; frozen at 2019 data permanently.
NCQA report cards as a stars preview — same-HEDIS-year ratings post ~3 weeks before CMS stars, but with no contract IDs, rating-scale only, and restrictive licensing.
Contract-level disability / dual percentages — CMS computes them internally; the public proxies are the LIS files, the SNP report, and the banded CAI adjustment categories inside the star zips.
State insurance-department complaint indexes for MA — the whole family fails on federal preemption: MA member complaints route to CMS (CTM/1-800-MEDICARE), not state DOIs. Verified: NAIC CIS has no free bulk export (per-company search UI; bulk products are paid) and is jurisdictionally thin on MA; Texas TDI's excellent open dataset has zero Medicare rows (no MA coverage line); California DMHC's IMR/complaint data excludes MA (appeals go to the federal IRE); NY DFS's health-insurer complaint rankings explicitly exclude Medicare.
Drug-shortage history (full event archive) — ASHP's database (richer than FDA's) is paid-license only, RSS is current-snapshot only; FDA's legacy shortage site is decommissioned (404) and the HealthData.gov export is a stale 2021 stub with zero columns. openFDA (Bucket 3) is the only live free feed, and it purges resolved records after ~1 year — a complete historical start/end panel does not exist publicly; Wayback reconstruction is the only route.
MA MLR QIA spend as a quality lead — exists (detailed-year §4 lines) but tested NOT predictive of subsequent star movement (rho ≈ 0 within rating band; negative level-correlation = remediation spend). Recorded so nobody re-mines it.
